There are several options for managing an investment property, including:
  • Self-management: The property owner manages the property themselves, handling tasks such as tenant screenings, rent collection, and maintenance.
  • Hiring a property management company: The property owner hires a professional property management company to handle tasks such as tenant screenings, rent collection, and maintenance.
  • Hiring a property manager: The property owner hires a single property manager to handle tasks such as tenant screenings, rent collection, and maintenance.
Ultimately, the decision of who will manage the property will depend on the property owner's preferences and their level of experience managing properties. It is important to discuss the pros and cons of each option and help the client make an informed decision that best fits their needs.

When you buy an investment property, the process of becoming a landlord can vary depending on a number of factors. Here are a few things to consider:
  • Finding tenants: Finding tenants can take anywhere from a few days to a few months, depending on the rental market and the condition of the property.
  • Legal paperwork: Depending on the state and local laws, there may be certain legal paperwork that needs to be completed before a tenant can move in. This can take a few days to a few weeks.
  • Inspections: Depending on the condition of the property, inspections may be required before a tenant can move in. This can take a few days to a few weeks.
  • Repairs and renovations: If the property needs repairs or renovations before it can be rented, this can take anywhere from a few days to a few months.
  • Closing on the property: It takes usually around 30-45 days to close on a property after a purchase agreement is signed.
Overall, the process of becoming a landlord after buying an investment property can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months, depending on the factors mentioned above.
